Tannin

Leathery

Definition

Savory, slightly tough aroma/texture reminiscent of leather — often with age or rustic reds.

Why this matters in wine

Bridges to earthy and tertiary notes.

FAQ

What does leathery mean in wine?
Savory, slightly tough aroma/texture reminiscent of leather — often with age or rustic reds.
Why does leathery matter for pairing?
Bridges to earthy and tertiary notes.
What is leathery often confused with?
Tasters often overlap leathery with earthy, tobacco. Each descriptor has a distinct place in the wine vocabulary.
