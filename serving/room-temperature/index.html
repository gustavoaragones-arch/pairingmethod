Room Temperature

Serving Temperature

Room temperature for wine means cool room — about 60–65°F (15–18°C), not ambient summer heat. Full-bodied reds like Cabernet Sauvignon, Merlot, Syrah, and Nebbiolo need this range for tannin, oak, and fruit to integrate.

Also known as: 60–65°F, 15–18°C

FAQ

Why is room temperature misleading for red wine?
Modern heated homes run 68–72°F+, which exaggerates alcohol and tannin. Aim for 60–65°F — a cool room or brief fridge rest before serving.
