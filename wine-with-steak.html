Best Wine for Steak

The best wines for steak are full-bodied reds with structure and tannin: Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, and Syrah. Tannins bind to protein and fat, softening the wine and highlighting the meat. Choose Cabernet for classic grilled steak, Malbec for richer cuts, and Syrah for pepper or herb crusts. Avoid light reds and most whites — they get lost against the dish.

Recommended Wines

Cabernet Sauvignon

Full-bodied, tannic, dark fruit. Classic steak pairing.

Malbec

Plush, fruity, smooth tannins. Great with ribeye and fat.

Syrah / Shiraz

Bold, peppery, structured. Fits herb and pepper crusts.

Why It Works

Steak is high in protein and fat. Red wines with noticeable tannin interact with both: tannins bind to protein, reducing astringency and making the wine feel smoother, while the wine’s structure stands up to the richness of the meat. Acidity and fruit balance the dish without overwhelming it.

Wines to Avoid

Light reds (e.g. Pinot Noir in a delicate style) and most white wines tend to be overshadowed by steak’s weight and can taste thin or sharp. Very oaky or overly sweet wines can clash with the savory, charred character of the meat. Stick to structured, dry reds for best results.

Preparation Variations

Grilled or pan-seared: Cabernet Sauvignon and Syrah match char and crust. Blue cheese or peppercorn sauce: Bolder Cabernet or Syrah hold up. Lighter preparations or filet: A structured Merlot or Malbec can work well. Adjust wine weight to sauce and cooking intensity.

Frequently Asked Questions

What red wine goes best with steak?
Cabernet Sauvignon, Malbec, and Syrah are among the best choices. They have enough tannin and body to pair with the protein and fat in steak.
Can you drink white wine with steak?
Most white wines are too light for steak and get overshadowed. Full-bodied whites (e.g. oaked Chardonnay) can work with lighter beef preparations but are not the default recommendation.
Does Malbec go with steak?
Yes. Malbec’s plush fruit and smooth tannins pair well with steak, especially richer cuts like ribeye, and are a classic choice in steakhouse settings.
